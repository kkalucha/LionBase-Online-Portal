Databases - Case¶

Problem¶

EarthX is a research instutition that studies all things related to climate. They are currently studying hydrology patterns across a river with 21 river channels, numbered 1 to 21, to understand how climate change is impacting the river. They would like to focus on River Channel 1 and compare its water cycle pattern to the river's primary watershed. River Channel 1 is the furthest from the watershed out of all the channels. EarthX would like to focus on information starting in 1990, when it was first reported that the planet had warmed by 0.5 degrees Celsius within the prior century.

Some terminology:

  • PREC and PRECIP refers to precipitation
  • PET refers to potential evapotranspiration which is the amount of evaporation that would occur given a sufficient water source
  • A watershed is a basin where precipitation collects and drains into a river
  • RCH revers to River Channel
  • mm is milimeters

EarthX has provided a SQLite database and asked you to do a preliminary analysis and come up with a proposal for a project.

In this case, you will learn how to:

  • Query data from SQL
  • Do basic data analysis
  • Formulate a proposal

Step 5: Create New Metric¶

The water cycle involves both precipitation and evaporation, which are measured in PREC and PET, respectively. How could we condense this down to one metric?

  • Let's take the difference between the two: PET_mm - PREC_mm and call it PED_mm
  • This tells us how much water that is evaporated into the air rains back down
  • We can add this as a column in our query

watershed_filtered =pd.read_sql_query("SELECT YR, MO, PREC_mm, PET_mm, PET_mm-PREC_mm as PED_mm \

However, can this be matched to the river channels? After speaking with EarthX, they confirm that SUB and RCH refer to the same channels by year and month.

So, we can join the two together to get the missing information.

Step 8: Join RCH and SUB¶

We apply an inner join as we only want information on channels that are included in both. We combine on the RCH and SUB fields along with each tables' YR and MO fields.

We also need to make sure we select data where the YR field for the RCH table is greater than or equal to 1990 and where we are referring to River Channel 1.

In [0]:
rch_sub_join = pd.read_sql_query("SELECT RCH, rch.YR, rch.MO, EVAPcms, PRECIPmm, PETmm, PETmm-PRECIPmm as RCH_PEDmm \
                                  FROM rch INNER JOIN sub \
                                  ON rch.RCH = sub.SUB and rch.YR=sub.YR and rch.MO=sub.MO WHERE rch.YR >= 1990\
                                  and RCH = 1", cnx)
